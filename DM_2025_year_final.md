## Q1 A. Describe in brief the following terms/concepts – Risk, Vulnerability, Hazard, Preparedness and Mitigation Measures (5 marks)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Risk** | Probability of loss or damage due to a hazard affecting vulnerable population. Formula: Risk = (Hazard × Exposure × Vulnerability) / Capacity |
| **Vulnerability** | Degree to which a system, community, or individual is susceptible to harm from a hazard. Types: physical, social, economic, environmental, institutional. |
| **Hazard** | Dangerous condition or event that threatens life, property, or environment. Types: natural (earthquake), man-made (Bhopal gas), socio-natural (flood). |
| **Preparedness** | Ongoing process of planning, training, and drills to ensure effective response. Examples: fire drills, emergency kits, evacuation plans. |
| **Mitigation Measures** | Actions to reduce disaster impact. Structural (embankments, retrofitting) and non-structural (building codes, land-use planning). |

## Q1 B. Explain how to communicate Cyclone information using 2 modes of communication for maximum reach (5 marks)

**Scenario:** Cyclone to hit coastal city in 72 hours.

**Mode 1: Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA) / Cell Broadcast**
- Method: Geo-targeted text messages sent to all mobile phones in affected area via cell towers
- Content: Cyclone name, expected landfall time, wind speed, evacuation instructions
- Reach: All phone users (no subscription needed), free, instant
- Example: IMD alerts for Cyclone Nisarga (2020) – reached millions in Mumbai

**Mode 2: Outdoor Public Warning Systems (Sirens + Loudspeakers)**
- Method: Fixed sirens in coastal areas + mobile loudspeaker vans in interior areas
- Content: Warning signal (continuous siren means evacuate), followed by announcements
- Reach: People without phones, fishermen on boats, illiterate population
- Example: Cyclone Phailin (2013) – sirens used in Odisha coastal villages

**Additional modes (if needed):** TV/radio broadcasts, social media (WhatsApp, Twitter), announcements at railway stations/bus stands, door-to-door by volunteers.

## Q2 B. Describe any 4 mitigation measures for prevention of Landslides. Also state 4 key causes of Landslides (10 marks)

**4 Mitigation Measures for Landslides:**

| Measure | Explanation | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1. **Retaining walls** | Concrete or stone walls constructed at slope toe to hold back soil/rock | Roads in Himalayan region (NH 58) |
| 2. **Drainage systems** | Surface and subsurface drains to prevent water saturation (water increases weight and reduces friction) | Catch drains, perforated pipes on slopes |
| 3. **Afforestation** | Planting trees with deep root systems to bind soil and increase slope stability | Vetiver grass, bamboo on slopes in Western Ghats |
| 4. **Slope grading / Terracing** | Reducing slope angle through excavation or creating steps (terraces) to reduce gravitational force | Agricultural terraces in Himachal Pradesh |

**4 Key Causes of Landslides:**

| Cause | Explanation | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1. **Heavy rainfall** | Increases pore water pressure, adds weight to slope, reduces friction | 2013 Kedarnath – triggered by cloudburst |
| 2. **Deforestation** | Removal of trees eliminates root cohesion, soil becomes loose | Western Ghats – clearing for plantations |
| 3. **Earthquakes** | Ground shaking destabilizes slopes | 2005 Kashmir earthquake triggered thousands of landslides |
| 4. **Road construction / Mining** | Cutting slopes, blasting, undercutting toe of slope | NH construction in Uttarakhand |

## Q3 A. Describe need for Ethical Principles in Disaster Management and explain any 8 ethical principles (10 marks)

**Need for Ethical Principles in Disaster Management:**

| Need | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| Prevents discrimination | Ensures relief reaches all affected equally regardless of caste, religion, gender |
| Protects vulnerable groups | Elderly, children, disabled, poor often neglected – ethics forces attention |
| Maintains trust | Public trusts relief agencies only if they act fairly and transparently |
| Prevents exploitation | Stops misuse of funds, sexual exploitation, forced labor in disaster zones |
| Upholds human dignity | Treats victims as humans with rights, not as helpless beneficiaries |

**8 Ethical Principles in Disaster Response:**

| S.No | Principle | Explanation |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Humanity** | Treat all victims humanely. Save lives first. Respect dignity. |
| 2 | **Impartiality** | Provide relief based on need alone – no discrimination by nationality, race, religion, class |
| 3 | **Neutrality** | Do not take sides in political, religious, or ideological conflicts |
| 4 | **Non-discrimination** | Equal treatment regardless of age, gender, disability, ethnicity, socioeconomic status |
| 5 | **Dignity** | Respect privacy and cultural practices. Avoid humiliating distribution methods. |
| 6 | **Solidarity** | Nations and communities cooperate; share resources to help victims |
| 7 | **Do no harm** | Avoid actions that worsen the situation (e.g., unsafe rescue, contaminated water) |
| 8 | **Accountability** | Transparent use of funds. Report to donors and affected communities. |

## Q3 B. Prepare checklist of 10 key considerations for Planning of Fire Drill in an Institution Building (10 marks)

**Fire Drill Planning Checklist:**

| S.No | Consideration | Details |
|------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | **Risk assessment** | Identify fire hazards (electrical panels, kitchen, library, lab). Map high-risk zones. |
| 2 | **Clear evacuation routes** | Ensure all exits marked, unlocked, unobstructed. Post floor-wise evacuation maps. |
| 3 | **Assembly point** | Designate safe outdoor area away from building. Ensure it is large enough for all occupants. |
| 4 | **Alarm system** | Test fire alarms and public address system before drill. Ensure audible in all areas. |
| 5 | **Assign roles** | Floor wardens, safety officers, first aiders, roll-call takers. Train them before drill. |
| 6 | **Special needs** | Plan for elderly, disabled, pregnant women, visitors who don't know building layout. |
| 7 | **Communication** | Walkie-talkies for coordinators. Backup plan if alarm fails (whistles, megaphones). |
| 8 | **Timing & notification** | Schedule drill; inform local fire brigade; notify occupants in advance (unannounced drill better for realism). |
| 9 | **Evacuation practice** | Practice "crawl low under smoke", feel doors before opening, use stairs (never lifts). |
| 10 | **Post-drill evaluation** | Measure evacuation time. Debrief participants. Identify gaps. Improve plan. Document lessons. |

## Q4 A. Describe any 6 mitigation measures adopted by Governance after "Gujarat Earthquake (2001)" for earthquakes (10 marks)

**Case Study: Gujarat Earthquake (26 Jan 2001, Bhuj – Magnitude 7.7, 20,000+ deaths)**

**6 Mitigation Measures adopted post-2001:**

| S.No | Measure | Explanation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Gujarat State Disaster Management Authority (GSDMA)** | Created exclusively for reconstruction and long-term disaster management. Model for other states. |
| 2 | **Owner-driven reconstruction** | Cash assistance to house owners to rebuild with earthquake-resistant designs (technical guidance provided). 8 lakh houses rebuilt. |
| 3 | **Building code enforcement** | Gujarat became first state to enforce National Building Code (NBC) strictly. Seismic Zone V areas (Kutch) – mandatory compliance. |
| 4 | **Retrofitting of public buildings** | Schools, hospitals, and government buildings strengthened using retrofitting techniques (jacketing, base isolation). |
| 5 | **School safety program** | Earthquake drills, awareness, and structural audits for all schools in high-risk zones. |
| 6 | **Seismic zoning map revision** | BIS updated zoning map; Kutch confirmed as Zone V. New townships planned away from fault lines. |

**Additional measures (for reference):**
- Training of engineers and masons in earthquake-resistant construction
- Public awareness campaigns (Do's & Don'ts, Drop Cover Hold)
- Creation of NDRF (National Disaster Response Force) – catalyzed by Gujarat experience

**Role of each component:**

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **CWC (Central Water Commission)** | Operates flood forecasting network (400+ stations). Issues real-time river level forecasts and inundation maps. |
| **IMD (India Meteorological Department)** | Provides rainfall forecasts, cyclone warnings, and extreme weather alerts. Essential for flood prediction. |
| **NDMA** | Lays down policies, plans, and guidelines for flood management. Coordinates national response. |
| **State SDMA & Water Resources Dept** | Implements flood control projects (embankments, dams, drainage). Issues state-level warnings and evacuation orders. |
| **District DDMA** | Local implementation – evacuation, relief distribution, shelter management, rescue coordination. |

**How inter-linking of rivers reduces flood risk:**

| Mechanism | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Transfer of excess water** | Surplus water from flood-prone rivers (e.g., Ganga, Brahmaputra) transferred to deficit areas (e.g., Penna, Krishna) via canals |
| **Reduced peak flow** | Diverting water before it reaches flood-prone downstream areas reduces river water level |
| **Reservoir storage coordination** | Linked reservoirs can store flood water in one basin and release gradually into another basin |
| **Example (India)** | Ken-Betwa link project – aims to transfer flood water from Ken to Betwa, reducing floods in Bundelkhand and providing irrigation |

**Limitations:** Inter-linking is expensive, requires inter-state agreements, and has environmental concerns.

## Q6 A. Describe how GIS can help in Hazard Mapping for Droughts with relevant examples (10 marks)

**GIS applications for Drought Hazard Mapping:**

| Application | Explanation | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 1. **Rainfall deviation mapping** | GIS overlays actual rainfall over normal rainfall maps to show deficit areas | IMD rainfall data + GIS → shows meteorological drought zones |
| 2. **SPI (Standardized Precipitation Index) mapping** | Calculates rainfall probability over time scales (1, 3, 6, 12 months) | GIS generates color-coded drought severity maps (red = severe) |
| 3. **NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)** | Satellite-based vegetation health monitoring – low NDVI = agricultural drought | MODIS/NOAA data + GIS → crop stress maps for Maharashtra (2016) |
| 4. **Groundwater level mapping** | GIS maps water table depth across districts using well data | CGWB data + GIS → groundwater drought zones |
| 5. **Soil moisture mapping** | Satellite soil moisture data (SMOS, AMSR-E) integrated with GIS | Identifies agricultural drought before crop failure |
| 6. **Vulnerability assessment** | Overlay drought hazard + population + livestock + irrigation data | Identifies districts needing priority drought relief |

**Case Study Example: Maharashtra Drought 2016**
- GIS used by Maharashtra Remote Sensing Application Centre (MRSAC)
- Generated drought hazard maps by combining: rainfall deficit (IMD), NDVI (satellite), groundwater levels (CGWB)
- Maps guided government: water tanker deployment, crop insurance claims, employment generation (MGNREGA) targeting

## Q7 A. Explain 5 key causes of Forest Fires and 5 immediate response measures (10 marks)

**5 Key Causes of Forest Fires:**

| Cause | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| 1. **Human negligence (90% in India)** | Cigarette butts, beedi ends, campfires left unattended, agricultural residue burning |
| 2. **Lightning** | Natural cause – common in dry thunderstorms |
| 3. **Arson / deliberate** | Poachers to drive animals, land clearing, revenge, or collection of forest produce (mahua, tendu) |
| 4. **Power lines / electrical sparks** | Frayed wires touching dry vegetation during windy conditions |
| 5. **Spontaneous combustion** | Very dry organic matter (leaves, peat) can self-ignite at high temperatures (rare) |

**5 Immediate Response Measures for Forest Fire Control:**

| Measure | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| 1. **Create fire lines / firebreaks** | Clear vegetation in a strip (3-10 m wide) to stop fire spread. Use bulldozers, manual clearing, or back burning. |
| 2. **Aerial firefighting** | Helicopters with Bambi buckets (water) or fire retardant chemicals. Essential for inaccessible terrain. |
| 3. **Ground teams with beaters** | Local villagers, forest staff, volunteers use green branch beaters to smother fire. Most common method in India. |
| 4. **Back burning / controlled burning** | Set small controlled fire ahead of main fire to remove fuel, so main fire stops when it reaches burned area. |
| 5. **Water spraying** | Fire engines with hoses where roads reach. Portable pumps from streams/ponds. |

## Q7 B. As a consultant for industry, recommend preparedness strategies for Gas Leaks – 6 strategies with justification (10 marks)

**Scenario:** Industry consultant recommending gas leak preparedness strategies.

| S.No | Strategy | Justification |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Install gas detection sensors** | Early warning before leak becomes dangerous. Sensors at storage tanks, pipelines, and processing units. Justification: Bhopal had no functioning sensors – detection within seconds saves lives. |
| 2 | **Multiple redundant safety systems** | Defense in depth – scrubbers, flare towers, water curtains, refrigeration. Justification: In Bhopal, all safety systems were non-functional. Redundancy ensures one failure doesn't cause disaster. |
| 3 | **Regular safety audits & maintenance** | Weekly/monthly inspection of valves, pipes, storage tanks. Maintenance logs. Justification: Bhopal had staff cuts and no preventive maintenance. Regular audits catch problems early. |
| 4 | **On-site emergency plan & drills** | Written plan with roles, evacuation routes, mock drills every 6 months. Justification: Bhopal had no emergency plan. Drills train workers to respond correctly (move upwind, don't use switches). |
| 5 | **Off-site emergency plan with community** | Coordinate with local administration, hospital, police. Sirens, public address, community training. Justification: Bhopal community had no warning or training. Off-site plan ensures public knows "cover nose with wet cloth, evacuate upwind". |
| 6 | **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)** | Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA), gas masks, protective suits for workers. Justification: Bhopal workers had no PPE. SCBA allows safe entry for leak containment. |

**Additional Justification Summary:** All strategies address failures from Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984) – the worst industrial disaster. Proactive preparedness prevents repeat of 15,000-20,000 deaths.
