## Q2 A. Discuss any 5 considerations for designing an Awareness Program for Fire Outbreak Preparedness (10 marks)

| S.No | Consideration | Explanation |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Target audience identification** | Different programs for school children, office workers, factory staff, rural communities. Example: School program uses cartoons/drills; factory program uses technical training. |
| 2 | **Simple, clear messaging** | Avoid technical jargon. Use local language. Focus on key do's/don'ts: "Stop, Drop, Roll" – "Crawl low under smoke" – "Feel doors before opening" |
| 3 | **Multiple communication channels** | Use posters, pamphlets, social media, radio, TV, local announcements. Example: Fire safety week in October – awareness campaigns across media. |
| 4 | **Hands-on training & demonstrations** | People remember doing more than reading. Demonstrate fire extinguisher use (PASS technique). Conduct mock fire drills. |
| 5 | **Regular repetition & reinforcement** | One-time awareness is forgotten. Annual fire drills, quarterly refresher posters, monthly safety meetings. Example: Schools conduct fire drills every 6 months. |

**Additional considerations:** Feedback collection, involvement of local fire brigade, distribution of emergency contact numbers.

## Q3 A. Illustrate with neat sketch any 2 types of Landslides and their specific mitigation measures (10 marks)

**Sketch 1: Rotational Slide (Slump)**

```
        Original ground surface
        ──────────────────
                   ╲
        Scarp ──────╲
        (steep face) ╲
                     ╲  Concave slip surface
                      ╲ (spoon-shaped)
                       ╲
                        └───── Toe (bulging)
```

**Rotational Slide (Slump):**
- Movement along concave (curved) slip surface
- Upper part tilts backward; lower part bulges outward
- Common in clay-rich, homogeneous slopes

**Mitigation measures for Rotational Slide:**
| Measure | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| 1. Improve drainage | Surface and subsurface drains to reduce pore water pressure |
| 2. Slope regrading | Reduce slope angle (cut and fill) |
| 3. Retaining walls | Concrete walls at toe to prevent bulging |
| 4. Avoid loading top of slope | No construction on upper part |

---

**Sketch 2: Debris Flow**

```
        Source area (loose soil, rock)
              │
              ▼
        Channel / Gully
              │
              ▼ (fast flow)
        Fan-shaped deposit at base
```

**Debris Flow:**
- Mixture of soil, rock, water flows like liquid (slurry)
- Very fast (up to 35 mph), carries large objects (trees, boulders)
- Common in steep drainages after heavy rainfall

**Mitigation measures for Debris Flow:**
| Measure | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| 1. Check dams | Small dams across gullies to trap debris |
| 2. Deflection barriers | Walls to redirect flow away from settlements |
| 3. Vegetation cover | Deep-rooted plants bind soil on slopes |
| 4. Early warning | Rainfall threshold alerts for evacuation |

## Q4 A. Analysing "Indian Ocean Tsunami 2004" – how Warning Systems and Drone Technology can be adopted for Preparedness and Response (10 marks)

**Case Study: Indian Ocean Tsunami (26 Dec 2004)** – No warning system existed; ~230,000 deaths across 14 countries.

**Warning Systems Adoption:**

| Warning System | How it helps | Example from post-2004 |
|----------------|--------------|------------------------|
| **Seismic network** | Detects undersea earthquakes in real-time (within 2-5 minutes) | INCOIS receives data from Indian Ocean seismic stations |
| **Bottom Pressure Recorders (BPRs)** | Detects tsunami waves in deep ocean (pressure changes) | BPRs deployed in Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea |
| **Tide gauges** | Confirms sea level rise near coast before waves arrive | Coastal tide gauge network expanded |
| **Satellite communication** | Rapid alert dissemination to control rooms | INSAT satellites broadcast warnings |
| **Community-based warning** | Sirens, public address systems, and natural signs training (earthquake + sea retreat = run) | Coastal villages in Tamil Nadu now have tsunami drills |

**Drone Technology Adoption:**

| Drone Application | How it helps | Example |
|-------------------|--------------|---------|
| **Rapid damage assessment** | Before rescue teams arrive, drones map flooded areas, collapsed buildings | Post-2004, drones could have mapped Nagapattinam damage within hours |
| **Search for stranded survivors** | Thermal cameras detect body heat in debris or on rooftops | Locating people in flooded areas inaccessible by boat |
| **Communication relay** | Drones act as temporary mobile towers when cellular networks fail | Andaman & Nicobar islands – communication was down for days |
| **Supply delivery** | Drop emergency supplies (water, medicine, life jackets) to isolated areas | Remote islands in Andaman chain |
| **Reconstruction planning** | High-resolution imagery for rebuilding coastal infrastructure | Mapping areas for relocation (buffer zones) |

**Conclusion:** If warning system existed in 2004, thousands of lives could have been saved. Drones accelerate response and reduce risk to rescue personnel.

## Q4 B. Critically evaluate five preparedness measures for mitigating cyclones and tsunamis in coastal areas (10 marks)

**Cyclone Preparedness Measures:**

| Measure | Evaluation (Effectiveness + Challenges) |
|---------|------------------------------------------|
| 1. **Cyclone shelters** | **Effective** – Odisha's 800+ shelters saved lives during Phailin (2013). **Challenge** – Maintenance, capacity during COVID, distance from villages. |
| 2. **Coastal embankments** | **Effective** – Reduces storm surge flooding. **Challenge** – Breaches (Kosi embankment failure), expensive to maintain. |
| 3. **Mangrove plantation** | **Highly effective** – Natural bio-shield (reduces wave energy 70-90%). **Challenge** – Takes years to grow, land encroachment. |
| 4. **Early warning & evacuation drills** | **Very effective** – IMD 72/48/24 hour warnings. **Challenge** – Warning fatigue, people refuse to evacuate (as seen in some cyclones). |
| 5. **Building codes (wind-resistant)** | **Effective** – Roof anchors, reinforced concrete reduce damage. **Challenge** – Poor enforcement in rural areas; cost of compliance. |

**Tsunami Preparedness Measures:**

| Measure | Evaluation |
|---------|------------|
| 1. **Tsunami early warning system (BPRs + seismic)** | **Very effective** – Provides 10-60 minute warning. **Challenge** – Detection from distant sources >3 hours, local tsunami (within 100 km) has minutes only. |
| 2. **Natural signs training** | **Essential** – For local tsunamis with no warning time. **Challenge** – Knowledge fades without regular drills. |
| 3. **Vertical evacuation structures** | **Effective** – Multi-story buildings on coast. **Challenge** – Expensive; not practical in all areas. |
| 4. **Mangrove preservation** | **Effective** – Reduces wave energy. **Challenge** – Same as cyclones. |
| 5. **Land-use zoning (no construction within 500 m of shore)** | **Effective** – Reduces exposure. **Challenge** – Political pressure, fishing communities need coast access. |

**Critical Conclusion:** Cyclones have **longer warning time** (24-72 hours) – systematic evacuation possible. Tsunamis have **very short warning** (minutes for local) – community training on natural signs is most critical.

## Q5 B. Explain how global climate change is linked to increasing frequency and intensity of Cyclones and Floods (10 marks)

**Mechanism:** Climate change → warmer air and ocean → more energy for cyclones → more moisture for floods.

**For Cyclones:**

| Link | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Warmer sea surface temperatures | Cyclones need >26.5°C; Arabian Sea now 30-31°C | Cyclone Nisarga (2020) – first to hit Mumbai since 1948 |
| Increased ocean heat content | More energy for intensification | Cyclone Amphan (2020) – rapid intensification to super cyclone in 24 hours |
| Slower cyclone movement | Cyclones linger longer over areas | Cyclone Fani (2019) – stalled over Odisha, caused more damage |
| Higher rainfall rates | Warmer air holds 7% more moisture per 1°C | Cyclone Ockhi (2017) – unprecedented rainfall in Kerala |

**For Floods:**

| Link | Explanation | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Extreme rainfall events | 7% more moisture = heavier downpours | 2013 Uttarakhand – 375 mm in 24 hours (cloudburst) |
| Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOFs) | Himalayan glaciers melting → lakes burst | 2021 Chamoli flood – triggered by rock/ice avalanche |
| Sea level rise | Higher baseline for storm surge | Sundarbans – increased coastal flooding |
| Changed monsoon patterns | Delayed onset, concentrated in short bursts | 2018 Kerala floods – 300% above normal rainfall in days |

**Conclusion:** India is a climate change hotspot – both cyclones (Arabian Sea now cyclone-prone) and floods (extreme rainfall events increasing) require adaptation strategies.

## Q6 A. Differentiate between preparedness and mitigation measures for Floods and Droughts (10 marks)

**Preparedness vs Mitigation:**

| Parameter | Preparedness | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| **Timing** | Before disaster (ongoing) | Before disaster (long-term) |
| **Focus** | Planning, training, early warning | Reducing impact, preventing occurrence |
| **Nature** | Non-structural (mostly) | Structural + non-structural |

**Flood Management:**

| Measure | Type | Explanation |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Flood forecasting (CWC alerts)** | Preparedness | Real-time river level warnings |
| **Evacuation drills** | Preparedness | Practice moving to higher ground |
| **Emergency kit preparation** | Preparedness | Food, water, documents ready |
| **Embakments / Levees** | Mitigation | Physical barrier to stop floodwater |
| **Dams / Reservoirs** | Mitigation | Store excess water, control release |
| **Channelization (widening/deepening rivers)** | Mitigation | Increase carrying capacity |
| **Floodplain zoning** | Mitigation | Restrict construction in flood-prone areas |

**Drought Management:**

| Measure | Type | Explanation |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Drought early warning (IMD rainfall forecast)** | Preparedness | Predict deficit rainfall weeks in advance |
| **Water rationing plans** | Preparedness | Planned reduced supply during dry periods |
| **Public awareness (save water campaigns)** | Preparedness | Behavior change |
| **Rainwater harvesting** | Mitigation | Capture and store rainwater |
| **Watershed management (check dams)** | Mitigation | Recharge groundwater |
| **Drip irrigation** | Mitigation | Reduce agricultural water use by 50-70% |
| **Drought-resistant crops** | Mitigation | Millets, pulses instead of paddy |

**Key Difference Summary:**
- **Preparedness** = getting ready (training, warnings, kits)
- **Mitigation** = reducing future impact (structures, policies, land-use changes)
